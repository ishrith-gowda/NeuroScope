### About Section
```
Undergraduate researcher in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences (EECS) at UC Berkeley. I develop deep learning methods for brain MRI harmonization -- standardizing multi-institutional neuroimaging data so that scanner-induced domain shifts do not confound downstream clinical analysis.

My current work introduces SA-CycleGAN-2.5D, a self-attention CycleGAN architecture with tri-planar 2.5D context for multi-site MRI harmonization. Extensions include federated learning for privacy-preserving multi-site training, compression-aware harmonization, multi-domain translation across scanner types, and downstream segmentation transfer evaluation.

Research interests: medical image analysis, domain adaptation, generative adversarial networks, federated learning, neuroimaging.
```

I am an undergraduate researcher in the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Sciences (EECS) at UC Berkeley. My research focuses on developing deep learning methods for brain MRI harmonization -- standardizing multi-institutional neuroimaging data so that scanner-induced domain shifts do not confound downstream clinical analysis.

My current work introduces SA-CycleGAN-2.5D, a self-attention CycleGAN architecture with tri-planar 2.5D context for multi-site MRI harmonization. The approach processes three adjacent axial slices simultaneously across four MRI modalities (T1, T1ce, T2, FLAIR), using multi-scale self-attention in the generator bottleneck and CBAM attention in encoder/decoder paths. Extensions include federated learning for privacy-preserving multi-site training, compression-aware harmonization, multi-domain translation across scanner types, and downstream segmentation transfer evaluation.

I am broadly interested in medical image analysis, domain adaptation, generative models, and federated learning for healthcare applications.
